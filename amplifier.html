Miniature Audio Amplifier

An advanced compact audio amplification system leveraging discrete transistor technology

This project showcases a high-performance audio amplifier designed using discrete transistors and simulated in LTspice. The amplifier employs a multi-stage architecture, starting with a small-signal transistor for initial voltage amplification, followed by driver and output stages optimized for current delivery to an 8Ω speaker. The design integrates precision biasing and feedback networks to ensure linearity and minimize distortion.

Key features include:

  • Input Coupling Capacitor: Blocks DC offset from the audio source, ensuring signal integrity.
  • Push-Pull Output Stage: Utilizes complementary transistors to reduce crossover distortion and improve efficiency.
  • Thermal Stability: Biasing diodes (D1, D2) and feedback resistors maintain consistent performance across varying conditions.

Powered by a single 9V battery, this amplifier is ideal for portable applications, such as amplifying signals from microphones or small audio devices. The design was successfully demonstrated in a classroom setting, providing a hands-on learning experience for 25 students and illustrating key principles of analog electronics.

The project exemplifies the practical application of analog circuit design, offering insights into transistor operation, biasing techniques, and feedback control. Its simplicity and effectiveness make it a valuable educational tool for aspiring engineers.
